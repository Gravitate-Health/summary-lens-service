• Karvea is an angiotensin-II receptor antagonist used to treat high blood pressure (essential hypertension) and protect the kidneys in patients with high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and impaired kidney function.
  • It works by preventing the binding of angiotensin-II to receptors in blood vessels, causing them to relax and lower blood pressure.
  • Do not take Karvea if allergic to irbesartan or any other ingredients, more than 3 months pregnant, or have diabetes or impaired kidney function and are treated with a blood pressure lowering medicine containing aliskiren.
  • Warning and precautions: talk to your doctor before taking Karvea if you have excessive vomiting or diarrhea, kidney or heart problems, receive Karvea for diabetic kidney disease, or are going to have an operation.
  • Karvea is not recommended in early pregnancy, and must not be taken if more than 3 months pregnant, as it may cause serious harm to the baby.
  • Children and adolescents should not use Karvea as the safety and efficacy have not been fully established.
  • Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking other medicines, especially ACE-inhibitors, aliskiren, potassium supplements, or non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs.
  • Karvea can be taken with or without food, and the usual dose is 150 mg once a day, which may be increased to 300 mg once daily depending on blood pressure response.
  • Possible side effects include dizziness, feeling sick/vomiting, fatigue, increased potassium levels, and decreased levels of a protein in red blood cells.
  • Rare but serious side effects include allergic skin reactions, localized swelling of the face, lips, and/or tongue, and severe allergic reactions.
  • Store Karvea out of sight and reach of children, do not use after the expiry date, and do not throw away via wastewater or household waste.
