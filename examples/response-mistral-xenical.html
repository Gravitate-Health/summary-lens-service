• Xenical is a prescription medicine used for weight loss in overweight adults and obese adults with a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 30 kg/m² or more, or 27 kg/m² or more if they have other risk factors.
  • It works by preventing the absorption of dietary fat in the gut.
  • The recommended dose is one 120mg capsule taken three times a day with each main meal containing fat.
  • Treatment should be continued for at least 12 weeks and discontinued if you have not lost at least 5% of your initial body weight.
  • Common side effects include headache, abdominal pain/discomfort, urgent or increased need to open the bowels, flatulence (wind), oily discharge, and low blood sugar levels for some people with type 2 diabetes.
  • Serious side effects include allergic reactions, skin blistering, diverticulitis, bleeding from the back passage, increases in liver enzymes, hepatitis, gallstones, pancreatitis, oxalate nephropathy, and effects on clotting with anti-coagulants.
  • Keep out of reach of children and store at room temperature in original packaging to protect from light and moisture.```
