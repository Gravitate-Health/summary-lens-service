What is Biktarvy and what is it used for?

  • Biktarvy is a single tablet for the treatment of human immunodeficiency virus 1 (HIV-1) infection in adults, adolescents, and children 2 years of age and older, who weigh at least 14 kg.
  • It reduces the amount of HIV in the body, improving the immune system and reducing the risk of developing illnesses linked to HIV infection.

What you need to know before you take Biktarvy?

  • Do not take Biktarvy if you are allergic to its active substances or have liver problems or a history of liver disease.
  • Tell your doctor if you have kidney disease, hepatitis B or C, or are taking certain medicines that may interact with Biktarvy.
  • Children under 2 years of age or weighing less than 14 kg should not take Biktarvy.

How to take Biktarvy?

  • Take Biktarvy exactly as prescribed by your doctor.
  • There are two strengths of Biktarvy tablets, and your doctor will prescribe the appropriate tablet for your age and weight.
  • Take the tablet with or without food, and do not chew or crush it.
  • If you miss a dose, take it as soon as possible, but do not take two doses at once.

Possible side effects?

  • Common side effects include depression, abnormal dreams, headache, dizziness, diarrhea, feeling sick, and tiredness.
  • Uncommon side effects include anaemia, vomiting, stomach pain, and joint pain.
  • Rare side effects include Stevens-Johnson syndrome, osteonecrosis, and suicidal thoughts and suicide attempt.

How to store Biktarvy?

  • Keep Biktarvy out of the sight and reach of children.
  • Store it in the original package in order to protect from moisture.
  • Do not use it after the expiry date, and do not throw away any medicines via wastewater or household waste.
