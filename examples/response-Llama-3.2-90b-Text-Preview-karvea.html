• What Karvea is and what it is used for: Karvea is an angiotensin-II receptor antagonist used to treat high blood pressure (essential hypertension) and protect the kidneys in patients with high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and impaired kidney function.

  • What you need to know before you take Karvea: Do not take Karvea if you are allergic to irbesartan, more than 3 months pregnant, or have diabetes and impaired kidney function and are treated with aliskiren. Inform your doctor if you have kidney problems, heart problems, or are going to have an operation.

  • Contraindications: Karvea is not recommended in early pregnancy, and must not be taken if you are more than 3 months pregnant. It is also not recommended for children and adolescents, and mothers who are breast-feeding.

  • Interactions with other medicines: Inform your doctor if you are taking an ACE-inhibitor, aliskiren, potassium supplements, salt substitutes containing potassium, potassium-sparing medicines, medicines containing lithium, or repaglinide.

  • How to take Karvea: Take Karvea exactly as your doctor has told you, with or without food. The usual dose is 150 mg once a day, which may be increased to 300 mg once daily depending on blood pressure response.

  • Possible side effects: Common side effects include dizziness, feeling sick/vomiting, fatigue, and increased levels of potassium. Uncommon side effects include heart rate increased, flushing, cough, diarrhoea, and indigestion/heartburn.
