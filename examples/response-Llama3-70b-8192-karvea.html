What is Karvea and what it is used for:

  • Karvea is an angiotensin-II receptor antagonist used to treat high blood pressure (essential hypertension) and protect the kidney in patients with high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and laboratory evidence of impaired kidney function.

What you need to know before taking Karvea:

  • Do not take Karvea if you are allergic to irbesartan or any other ingredients, more than 3 months pregnant, or have diabetes or impaired kidney function and are treated with a blood pressure lowering medicine containing aliskiren.
  • Talk to your doctor before taking Karvea if you have kidney problems, heart problems, diabetes, or are going to have an operation or be given anaesthetics.

How to take Karvea:

  • Take Karvea exactly as your doctor has told you, with or without food, and at the same time each day.
  • The usual dose is 150 mg once a day, but may be increased to 300 mg once daily depending on blood pressure response.

Possible side effects:

  • Common side effects include dizziness, feeling sick/vomiting, fatigue, and increased levels of potassium in the blood.
  • Uncommon side effects include heart rate increased, flushing, cough, diarrhoea, indigestion/heartburn, and sexual dysfunction.
  • Rare side effects include allergic skin reactions, localised swelling of the face, lips and/or tongue, and severe allergic reactions.
