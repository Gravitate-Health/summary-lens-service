• Possible side effects include systemic allergy (rare), local allergy (common), lipodystrophy (uncommon), and oedema.

  • Report any side effects to your doctor or pharmacist.

  • Common problems of diabetes include hypoglycaemia (low blood sugar) and hyperglycaemia (too much sugar in the blood).

  • Hypoglycaemia can be caused by taking too much insulin, missing meals, exercising too hard, having an infection or illness, or having trouble with kidneys or liver.

  • Hyperglycaemia can lead to diabetic ketoacidosis, which requires immediate medical attention.

  • If you are ill, your insulin needs may change, and you should test your urine or blood and follow your "sick rules" and inform your doctor.

  • Store Humalog in a refrigerator before first use, but not after. Keep the pen and cartridges at room temperature and discard after 28 days. Do not freeze or expose to heat or sunlight.

  • Check each time you inject yourself for clumps of material or solid white particles sticking to the bottom or wall of the cartridge.

  • Dispose of medicines no longer required in accordance with local regulations and guidelines.
