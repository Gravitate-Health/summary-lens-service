• What Humalog Mix50 is and what it is used for: Humalog Mix50 is used to treat diabetes, working more quickly than normal human insulin to control glucose levels in the blood.
  • Key characteristics: Humalog Mix50 is a substitute for the body's own insulin, works quickly and lasts a shorter time than soluble insulin (2 to 5 hours), and is suitable for use in adults and children.
  • Precautions before taking Humalog Mix50: Do not use if experiencing hypoglycaemia, allergic to insulin lispro or other ingredients, or if blood sugar levels are not well-controlled.
  • Important considerations: Rotate injection sites to prevent skin changes, inform doctor of recent illness, kidney or liver trouble, or changes in exercise or alcohol consumption.
  • How to take Humalog Mix50: Inject within 15 minutes of a meal, follow doctor's instructions for dosage and frequency, and use Lilly insulin pens with Humalog Mix50 cartridges.
  • Preparing and injecting Humalog Mix50: Rotate cartridges before use, do not shake vigorously, and follow instructions for preparing the pen and injecting the insulin.
  • Possible side effects: Systemic allergy, local allergy, lipodystrophy, oedema, and common problems of diabetes such as hypoglycaemia and hyperglycaemia.
  • Hypoglycaemia and hyperglycaemia: Recognize warning symptoms, take action to treat, and seek medical help if necessary.
  • Storage and disposal: Store in refrigerator before first use, keep in use at room temperature, and discard after 28 days; do not dispose of via wastewater or household waste.
