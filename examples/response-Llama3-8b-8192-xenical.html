What is Xenical and what is it used for?

  • Xenical is a medicine used to treat obesity
  • It works by blocking about one-third of the fat in food from being digested
  • It is indicated for use in conjunction with a low-calorie diet

What you need to know before taking Xenical

  • Do not take Xenical if you are allergic to orlistat or have chronic malabsorption syndrome, cholestasis, or are breast-feeding
  • Weight loss may affect the dose of medicines taken for other conditions
  • Follow a nutrition program recommended by your doctor to gain maximum benefit
  • Avoid over-consumption of fat and calories
  • Xenical may cause harmless changes in bowel habits, such as fatty or oily stools
  • Use of an additional contraceptive method is recommended to prevent possible failure of oral contraception
  • Xenical may be associated with renal stones in patients with chronic kidney disease

How to take Xenical

  • Take one 120mg capsule with each of the three main meals per day
  • Take with a well-balanced, calorie-controlled diet that is rich in fruit and vegetables and contains an average of 30% of calories from fat
  • Avoid intake of food containing fat between meals
  • Xenical only works in the presence of dietary fat

Possible side effects

  • Common side effects include headache, abdominal pain, urgent need to open bowels, flatulence, oily discharge, oily or fatty stools, and low blood sugar levels
  • Rare but serious side effects include allergic reactions, skin blistering, diverticulitis, bleeding from the back passage, increases in liver enzymes, hepatitis, gallstones, pancreatitis, and oxalate nephropathy

How to store Xenical

  • Keep out of the sight and reach of children
  • Do not use Xenical after the expiry date stated on the carton or bottle
  • Store in original package and keep the blister in the outer carton in order to protect from light and moisture
  • Medicines should not be disposed via wastewater or household waste.
