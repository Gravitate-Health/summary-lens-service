• Biktarvy is a medication used to treat HIV-1 infection in adults, adolescents, and children aged 2 years and older who weigh at least 14 kg.
  • It contains three active substances: bictegravir, emtricitabine, and tenofovir alafenamide.
  • Biktarvy is not a cure for HIV infection and while taking it, individuals may still develop illnesses associated with HIV infection.
  • Do not take Biktarvy if allergic to its components, currently taking rifampicin or St. Johnâ€™s wort, or if you have liver or kidney problems.
  • Inform your doctor if you have hepatitis B or C, as there is a higher risk of severe liver complications during treatment.
  • While taking Biktarvy, look out for signs of inflammation, infection, joint pain, stiffness, or bone problems.
  • Biktarvy may interact with other medications, so inform your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking any other medicines.
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding individuals should consult their doctor before taking Biktarvy.
  • Do not breastfeed during treatment, as some active substances pass into human breast milk.
  • If you miss a dose, take it as soon as possible if less than 18 hours have passed, or wait and take the next dose at your usual time if more than 18 hours have passed.
  • Do not stop taking Biktarvy without talking to your doctor, as stopping the medication can seriously affect future treatment.
  • Common side effects include depression, abnormal dreams, headache, dizziness, diarrhea, feeling sick, and tiredness.
  • Report any side effects to your doctor or pharmacist.
  • Store Biktarvy in its original package, keep it out of sight and reach of children, and do not use it after the expiry date.
