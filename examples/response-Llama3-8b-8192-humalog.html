What is Humalog Mix50 and what is it used for?

  • Humalog Mix50 is a medication used to treat diabetes
  • It is a substitute for the body's own insulin and is used to control blood sugar levels in the long term
  • It works quickly and lasts for a shorter time than soluble insulin (2-5 hours)

What you need to know before taking Humalog Mix50

  • Do not use Humalog Mix50 if you think you are experiencing hypoglycaemia (low blood sugar)
  • Check the pack and cartridge label for the name and type of insulin
  • Be aware of warning signs of hypoglycaemia, such as shakiness, rapid heartbeat, and sweating
  • Inform your doctor if you have recently become ill, have trouble with your kidneys or liver, or are exercising more than usual
  • Tell your doctor if you are taking other medications, including oral hypoglycaemics, steroids, and beta-blockers

How to take Humalog Mix50

  • Inject Humalog Mix50 under the skin within 15 minutes of a meal
  • Rotate the injection site to prevent skin changes
  • Check the cartridge frequently and do not use it if clumps of material are present
  • Follow the instructions for preparing and using the pen

Possible side effects

  • Systemic allergy: rash, blood pressure dropping, difficulty breathing, and heart beating fast
  • Local allergy: redness, swelling, or itching around the injection site
  • Lipodystrophy: shrinkage or thickening of fatty tissue at the injection site
  • Oedema: swelling in arms, ankles, or fluid retention

How to store Humalog Mix50

  • Store in a refrigerator (2°C-8°C) before first use
  • Keep at room temperature (below 30°C) and discard after 28 days
  • Do not freeze or expose to heat or sunlight
  • Keep out of the sight and reach of children
