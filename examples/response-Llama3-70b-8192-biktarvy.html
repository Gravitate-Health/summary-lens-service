What is Biktarvy and what is it used for?

  • Biktarvy is a single tablet containing three active substances: bictegravir, emtricitabine, and tenofovir alafenamide.
  • It is used to treat human immunodeficiency virus 1 (HIV-1) infection in adults, adolescents, and children 2 years of age and older, who weigh at least 14 kg.

What you need to know before taking Biktarvy

  • Do not take Biktarvy if you are allergic to any of its ingredients or if you are taking certain medicines, such as rifampicin or St. John's wort.
  • Inform your doctor if you have liver problems, hepatitis B infection, kidney disease, or if you are pregnant or breast-feeding.
  • Biktarvy may interact with other medicines, so inform your doctor about all medicines you are taking.

How to take Biktarvy

  • Take Biktarvy exactly as your doctor has told you, with or without food.
  • The recommended dose is one tablet per day, with the strength of the tablet depending on your age and weight.
  • Do not stop taking Biktarvy without talking to your doctor, as this can seriously affect how future treatment works.

Possible side effects

  • Common side effects include depression, abnormal dreams, headache, dizziness, diarrhea, feeling sick, and tiredness.
  • Uncommon side effects include anaemia, vomiting, stomach pain, and joint pain.
  • Rare side effects include Stevens-Johnson syndrome, a serious life-threatening condition.
  • Other possible side effects include bone problems, weight gain, and changes in blood lipids and glucose levels.

How to store Biktarvy

  • Keep Biktarvy out of the sight and reach of children.
  • Store the medicine in its original package, in a dry place, and do not use it after the expiry date.
  • Dispose of unused medicine according to the instructions of your pharmacist.
