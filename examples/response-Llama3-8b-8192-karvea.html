What Karvea is and what it is used for:

  • Karvea is an angiotensin-II receptor antagonist that lowers blood pressure and slows kidney function decline in patients with high blood pressure and type 2 diabetes.
  • It is used to treat high blood pressure (essential hypertension) and protect the kidney in patients with high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, and laboratory evidence of impaired kidney function.

What you need to know before you take Karvea:

  • Do not take Karvea if you are allergic to irbesartan or have kidney problems, heart problems, or diabetes-related kidney problems.
  • Inform your doctor if you have excessive vomiting or diarrhea, get low blood sugar levels, or are going to have an operation or be given anaesthetics.
  • Karvea is not recommended in early pregnancy, and must not be taken if you are more than 3 months pregnant.
  • Children and adolescents should not take Karvea.

How to take Karvea:

  • Take Karvea exactly as your doctor has told you, with or without food.
  • The usual dose is 150 mg once a day, which may be increased to 300 mg once daily depending on blood pressure response.
  • Patients with high blood pressure and type 2 diabetes with kidney disease should take 300 mg once daily.

Possible side effects:

  • Common side effects include dizziness, feeling sick/vomiting, fatigue, and raised levels of potassium.
  • Rare but serious side effects include allergic skin reactions, localised swelling of the face, lips, and/or tongue, and low blood sugar levels.
