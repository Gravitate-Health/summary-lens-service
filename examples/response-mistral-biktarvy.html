• Biktarvy is a prescription medicine used for the treatment of HIV infection in certain adults and adolescents.
  • It contains three active ingredients: bictegravir, emtricitabine, and tenofovir alafenamide.
  • Take one tablet once daily with or without food. Do not crush, chew, or split the tablet. Swallow it whole.
  • If you miss a dose, take it as soon as possible within 12 hours after the missed dose. Do not take two doses at the same time.
  • Tell your doctor if you have any kidney problems or are taking any other medicines, including herbal remedies and supplements.
  • Stop treatment and contact your doctor immediately if you experience any signs of inflammation or infection, muscle weakness, palpitations, tremor, or suicidal thoughts.
  • Common side effects include depression, abnormal dreams, headache, dizziness, diarrhoea, feeling sick (nausea), tiredness (fatigue).
  • Uncommon side effects include anaemia, vomiting, stomach pain, wind (flatulence), swelling of the face, lips, tongue or throat (angioedema), itching (pruritus), rash, hives (urticaria), joint pain (arthralgia).
  • Rare side effects include Stevens-Johnson syndrome.
  • During HIV therapy there may be an increase in weight and in levels of blood lipids and glucose.
  • Keep this medicine out of the sight and reach of children. Do not use after expiry date. Store in original package to protect from moisture.```
