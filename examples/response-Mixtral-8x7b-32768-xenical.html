• Xenical is a medicine used to treat obesity, blocking the digestion of about one-third of fat in consumed food.
  • It should be taken with a low calorie intake diet, distributed evenly over three main meals.
  • Do not take Xenical if allergic to orlistat or other ingredients, if you have chronic malabsorption syndrome, cholestasis, or are breast-feeding.
  • Inform your doctor if taking other medicines, as Xenical may modify their activity (e.g. anticoagulants, ciclosporin, iodine salts/levothyroxine, amiodarone, HIV medicines, anticonvulsants, acarbose).
  • Xenical can cause harmless changes in bowel habits and may unbalance anticonvulsant treatment.
  • Taking Xenical during pregnancy is not recommended, and it should not be taken while breast-feeding.
  • Common side effects include headache, abdominal pain, urgent/increased need to open the bowels, flatulence, oily discharge, and low blood sugar levels.
  • Report any side effects to your doctor or pharmacist.
  • Keep Xenical out of reach of children, store in original package, and do not dispose of in wastewater or household waste.
