• What is Biktarvy and what it is used for: Biktarvy is a single tablet containing three active substances (bictegravir, emtricitabine, and tenofovir alafenamide) used to treat human immunodeficiency virus 1 (HIV-1) infection in adults, adolescents, and children 2 years of age and older, who weigh at least 14 kg.

  • What you need to know before taking Biktarvy: Do not take Biktarvy if you are allergic to any of the ingredients, taking rifampicin or St. John’s wort, or have liver problems. Inform your doctor if you have liver disease, kidney disease, or are taking other medicines.

  • How to take Biktarvy: Always take Biktarvy exactly as your doctor has told you. The recommended dose is one tablet per day with or without food. Do not stop taking Biktarvy without talking to your doctor.

  • Possible side effects: Common side effects include depression, abnormal dreams, headache, dizziness, diarrhea, feeling sick, and tiredness. Rare but serious side effects include Stevens-Johnson syndrome, bone problems, and autoimmune disorders.

  • Interactions with other medicines: Inform your doctor if you are taking other medicines, including antacids, mineral supplements, and iron supplements. Do not take Biktarvy with rifampicin or St. John’s wort.

  • Pregnancy and breast-feeding: Inform your doctor if you are pregnant or breast-feeding. Do not breast-feed during treatment with Biktarvy.

  • Driving and using machines: Biktarvy can cause dizziness. Do not drive or ride a bicycle if you feel dizzy.

  • Storage: Keep Biktarvy out of the sight and reach of children. Store in the original package, protect from moisture, and keep the bottle tightly closed.
